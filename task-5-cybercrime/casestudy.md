PART A: CASE STUDY


Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking details by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that appears to be from a bank or a well-known company. The message often contains a link that directs the user to a fake website that looks similar to the original. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker collects this information and uses it for fraud.

Students and young internet users are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify links carefully. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links and sharing personal information online.
